The Majestic Cockatoo

The emu, also known by scientists as the Dromaius novaehollandiae, is the second-fastest bird in this world, only outrun by its cousin, the ostrich. As simple as emus look, they have a multitude of unique qualities about them. In this article, we will talk about some of them. So for some quick facts, they are classified as omnivores because of their diet. Their diet consists of seeds, fruits, flowers, shoots, insects, and even small vertebrae! Their average lifespan in the wild is a quite saddening 10-20 years with the longest living emu living to 38 years old. Now, for the interesting stuff. This bird, while flight-less, can run faster than Olympic Gold Medalist, Usain Bolt, who can run as fast as 27.33 miles per hour(43.99 kph) They also have quite powerful kicks, which are useful for keeping their predators at bay. Lastly, we will talk about their origins. Emus are a member of the ratite family, which also consists of ostriches, cassowaries, and rheas. They are among the largest birds on the planet. They are commonly found in the Australian Wildlands, but also New Guinea, Indonesia, and the Philippines. Regrettably, this is where we shall end this article.
